Teaching Applications of IOT to childrens

The two-day IoT workshop organized by the IEEE VIIT Student Branch at MES English Medium School in Shirwal on January 23rd and 24th was a resounding success, engaging students from classes 5th to 9th. The workshop focused on providing them with hands-on experience with IoT projects featuring sensors, Arduino boards, and online simulation tools such as TinkerCAD. They gained an understanding of the concept of IoT, exploring how everyday objects can be connected to the internet to collect and exchange data. The workshop highlighted various applications of IoT across different industries, showcasing how this technology is transforming sectors such as healthcare, agriculture, smart cities, and more. The projects not only reinforced theoretical concepts but also empowered the kids to experience the outcomes of their learning. By the end of the workshop, the students provided insightful inputs on the real-life applications of the projects they worked on, showcasing their understanding of IoT's practical implications. The event, organized by IEEE, aimed to inspire and prepare young minds for the evolving landscape of IoT technology. The workshop's success was marked by the active participation of IEEE members, recognizing the significance of this learning opportunity for both academic and professional growth.
